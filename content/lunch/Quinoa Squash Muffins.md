Protein and texture from the quinoa, sweet pockets from the squash and raisins. You can use any squash, cooked or shredded for these muffins. Enjoy. 

{{< boldheading >}}

{{< checkbox "1 cup water" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ cup uncooked quinoa, rinsed and drained" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ cups all-purpose flour" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 cup whole wheat flour" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ teaspoon salt" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ teaspoons baking powder" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 teaspoon baking soda" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 pinch ground cloves  (Optional)" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 pinch ground nutmeg  (Optional)" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 pinch ground allspice  (Optional)" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 pinch ground ginger  (Optional)" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 pinch cayenne pepper  (Optional)" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ cups shredded squash" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ cups plain yogurt" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ cup brown sugar" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ cup raisins" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ cup chopped walnuts" >}}
{{< checkbox "2  eggs" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 teaspoon vanilla extract" >}}


{{< direction >}}

**Step: 1**

Bring water and quinoa to a boil in a saucepan. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until quinoa is tender and water is absorbed, 15 to 20 minutes.{{< span >}}

**Step: 2**

Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Grease 18 muffin cups or line with paper muffin liners.{{< span >}}

**Step: 3**

Mix all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, cinnamon, baking powder, baking soda, cloves, nutmeg, allspice, ground ginger, and cayenne pepper together in a bowl.{{< span >}}

**Step: 4**

Stir squash, yogurt, brown sugar, raisins, walnuts, eggs, and vanilla together in a large bowl. Add flour mixture to squash mixture; stir. Spoon batter into prepared muffin cups.{{< span >}}

**Step: 5**

Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, 30 minutes.{{< span >}}
