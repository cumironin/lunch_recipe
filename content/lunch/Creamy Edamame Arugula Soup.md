Soup's on! Packed with high quality soy protein and fiber, this quick and easy homemade soup will warm your soul and keep you satisfied for hours. 

{{< boldheading >}}

{{< checkbox "1 teaspoon soybean oil (often labeled "vegetable oil")" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 small onion, diced" >}}
{{< checkbox "3 cups frozen edamame (shelled)" >}}
{{< checkbox "2 cups low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 cup plain soymilk" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 cup baby arugula leaves, packed" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ teaspoon salt, or to taste" >}}
{{< checkbox "¼ teaspoon ground black pepper" >}}
{{< checkbox "¼ teaspoon Greek-style yogurt" >}}


{{< direction >}}

**Step: 1**

Heat soybean oil in large saucepan over medium heat. Add onions and cook for 2 to 3 minutes, until soft. Add edamame and broth; simmer for 5 to 6 minutes, until just tender.{{< span >}}

**Step: 2**

Place mixture in food processor; add soymilk and arugula. Process 1 minute, or until smooth.{{< span >}}

**Step: 3**

Return to saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until soup begins to simmer. Ladle into bowls; top with yogurt, if desired.{{< span >}}

**Step: 4**

Thin soup with additional vegetable broth as needed.{{< span >}}
