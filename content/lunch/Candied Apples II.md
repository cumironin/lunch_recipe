Apples with a hard candy coating, like you can get at fairs. 

{{< boldheading >}}

{{< checkbox "15  apples" >}}
{{< checkbox "2 cups white sugar" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 cup light corn syrup" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ cups water" >}}
{{< checkbox "8 drops red food coloring" >}}


{{< direction >}}

**Step: 1**

Lightly grease cookie sheets.  Insert craft sticks into whole, stemmed apples.{{< span >}}

**Step: 2**

In a medium saucepan over medium-high heat, combine sugar, corn syrup and water.  Heat to 300 to 310 degrees F (149 to 154 degrees C), or until a small amount of syrup dropped into cold water forms hard, brittle threads.  Remove from heat and stir in food coloring.{{< span >}}

**Step: 3**

Holding apple by its stick, dip in syrup and remove and turn to coat evenly.  Place on prepared sheets to harden.{{< span >}}
