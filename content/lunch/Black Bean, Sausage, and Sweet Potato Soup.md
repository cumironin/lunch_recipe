An unusual blend of flavors create this flavorful soup. Garnish with fresh scallions. 

{{< boldheading >}}

{{< checkbox "5 ½ cups water" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ cups dry black beans" >}}
{{< checkbox "3 cloves garlic, minced" >}}
{{< checkbox "1  bay leaf" >}}
{{< checkbox "¼ teaspoon ground allspice" >}}
{{< checkbox "2 cups chicken broth" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 tablespoon tomato paste" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 tablespoon water" >}}
{{< checkbox "¼ pound Italian sausage, cut into 1/2 inch pieces" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 pound sweet potatoes" >}}
{{< checkbox "3  green onions" >}}
{{< checkbox "salt to taste" >}}
{{< checkbox "ground black pepper to taste" >}}


{{< direction >}}

**Step: 1**

In a 4 quart saucepan, combine beans, garlic, bay leaf, allspice, broth and 5 1/2 cups water. Cook, partially covered, until beans are tender, about 50 minutes. Discard bay leaf.{{< span >}}

**Step: 2**

In a blender puree 1 cup cooked beans with 1 cup cooking liquid, and return to pan.{{< span >}}

**Step: 3**

In a small bowl, stir together tomato paste and 1 tablespoon water; stir into beans with sausage and Worcestershire sauce. Simmer soup, covered, for 15 minutes. Soup may be prepared up to this point 3 days ahead.{{< span >}}

**Step: 4**

While soup is simmering, peel sweet potatoes and cut into 1/2 inch pieces. Steam until tender, about 10 minutes. Stir potatoes, 3 chopped scallions, and salt and pepper to taste into soup. Serve soup garnished with scallion greens.{{< span >}}
