This fruity salad is an ideal complement to pork. 

{{< boldheading >}}

{{< checkbox "1 teaspoon olive oil, or as needed" >}}
{{< checkbox "5  green onions, white parts only, chopped, or more to taste" >}}
{{< checkbox "4 cloves garlic, crushed" >}}
{{< checkbox "2 ¼ cups water" >}}
{{< checkbox "6  dates, pitted and chopped" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ (6 ounce) package sweetened dried cranberries (such as Craisins®)" >}}
{{< checkbox "½ cup golden raisins" >}}
{{< checkbox "¼ cup shelled peanuts" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 ½ cups pearl (Israeli) couscous" >}}
{{< checkbox "1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro, or more to taste" >}}
{{< checkbox "1  mango, peeled and chopped" >}}


{{< direction >}}

**Step: 1**

Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat; saute onion and garlic until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes. Add water to onion mixture and bring to a boil.{{< span >}}

**Step: 2**

Stir dates, cranberries, raisins, and peanuts into onion mixture and bring to a boil again. Add couscous and cilantro to dried fruit-onion mixture; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover skillet, and simmer until water is mostly absorbed, 10 to 15 minutes.{{< span >}}

**Step: 3**

Stir mango into couscous mixture, return cover, and simmer until all the water is absorbed and couscous is tender, about 5 more minutes.{{< span >}}
